## Problem Statement
Hoosier Gasoline Companny, or HoosCo as they are known, produces two blends of gasoline, regular and premium, by mixing three 
different types of oil, indicated by Types 1, 2, and 3. Each type of oil comes in barrels and has its own cost and octane readings,
which are given below.

|      | Cost / Barrel | Octane Rating | Maximum Available |
|------|:-------------:|:-------------:|:-----------------:|
|Type 1|            $45|             93|             10,000|
|Type 2|            $35|             91|             15,000|
|Type 3|            $20|             87|             20,000|

To produce each blend of gasoline, we must combine these oils so as to meet the following requirements.

| Blend            | Type 1       | Type 2       | Type 3       |
|------------------|-------------:|-------------:|-------------:|
| Regular gasoline | at least 25% | at least 35% | at least 30% |
| Premium gasoline | at least 30% | at least 45% | at least 20% |

There are also sales prices, average octane rating requirements for gasolines, and minimum requirements in 
production of each blend.

|                  | Average Octane Rating | Sales Price | Minimum Demand |
|------------------|:---------------------:|:-----------:|:--------------:|
| Regular gasoline |                     89|          $65|  15,000 barrels|
| Premium gasoline |                     91|          $75|  12,500 barrels|

How much of each type of gasoline should be produced to satisfy all requirements and maximize profits?
